Alan Turing

Alan Turing

The topic that interested me the most during the year was Alan Turing and computer science, because he was one of the first people who helped create the foundations of modern computers. Before studying him in depth, I knew his name but not what he had actually done — and what he did was extraordinary.

The man and his work

Breaking Enigma: during the Second World War, Turing worked at Bletchley Park, the British codebreaking centre. The German military used the Enigma machine to encrypt their communications. Turing led the team that built the Bombe, an electromechanical device that could test millions of possible key settings per day and find the right configuration to decrypt the messages. His work is estimated to have shortened the war by two to four years and saved millions of lives.

The Turing Machine: before the war, Turing had already developed the theoretical foundations of modern computing. He described a hypothetical machine — now called a Turing Machine — that could simulate any algorithm by reading and writing symbols on a tape following a set of rules. This abstract model proved that there exists a class of problems that no computer can ever solve, regardless of its power. It also defined what "computation" means at a fundamental level.

The Turing Test: in 1950 Turing published a paper asking "Can machines think?" He proposed a test: if a human interviewer cannot distinguish a machine's responses from a human's through a text conversation, the machine can be considered intelligent. This idea remains one of the central concepts in artificial intelligence research today.

The human story

Despite his extraordinary contributions, Turing was prosecuted by the British government in 1952 for his homosexuality, which was illegal at the time. He was forced to undergo chemical castration as an alternative to prison. He died in 1954, aged 41, in circumstances officially ruled as suicide. He received a royal pardon only in 2013 — almost sixty years after his death.

Ho scelto Alan Turing perché la sua storia mi ha colpito sia dal punto di vista tecnico che umano. Si collega naturalmente a quasi tutte le altre materie: all'informatica (fondamenti teorici della computazione), alla storia (Seconda Guerra Mondiale e crittografia), all'intelligenza artificiale (il Turing Test) e ai diritti civili.
